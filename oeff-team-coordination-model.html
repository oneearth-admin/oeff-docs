OEFF 2026 — Team Coordination

How Our Team Works Together

Three roles, designed to complement each other. No bottlenecks, full coverage, clear handoffs.

The festival's operational load has grown beyond what any single person can carry. This model distributes the work across three complementary roles so that decisions keep moving, hosts get answers quickly, and Ana can focus on relationships and strategic direction rather than routing every question herself.

The Design Principle

Garen builds the systems and coordinates the budget. Templates, documentation, specs, escalation triggers, data infrastructure, and day-to-day spending decisions within the approved allocation. The things that make work repeatable and financially legible.

Erin is the on-site presence. In-house media production, on-site AV point of contact during festival, weekly meeting bridge, contractor check-ins. The business-hours and boots-on-the-ground person the team needs.

Kim makes the whole thing legible. Maps the communication flows, designs the delegation playbooks, organizes the knowledge base. The connective tissue that keeps it working as people come and go.

Ana leads. Relationships, film matching, strategic direction, final calls. The things that require her specific judgment and trust — not routing, not logistics, not process questions.

What Changes for Ana

Before this model
  • Every host question routes through Ana
  • No one available during business hours for tech ops
  • New contractors figure things out on their own
  • Processes live in people's heads
  • Communication channels unclear — things get lost
With three roles working together
  • Routine questions answered same-day, without Ana
  • Business-hours coverage through Erin
  • Contractors onboarded with structure and check-ins
  • Processes documented and maintained
  • Clear routing — everyone knows where things go

What this means for Ana

  • Garen handles vendor invoices and routine spending up to $1,500 without needing your sign-off
  • Spending above $1,500 requires your approval before Garen acts
  • Garen’s own compensation and Erin’s contract are approved by you directly — outside Garen’s coordination authority
  • Monthly spend report from Garen — transparent, simple, one page
  • If anything approaches a cap or needs reallocation, Garen flags it before acting
All within the existing board-approved budget. No new money. Just cleaner accountability with clear approval boundaries.
