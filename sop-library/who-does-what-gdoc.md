## Detailed roles

### Ana — Executive Director — Year 15

**Handles:** Vision, budget, partnerships. Face and voice of the festival. The relationships that hold everything together.

**Defines:** Scope, contracts, festival-week logistics, hiring. Anything that commits the organization.

**Greenlights:** Public-facing comms, press, sponsor commitments, anything carrying the organization's name.

**Routing:**
- Systems and process route through **Garen**
- Creative production routes through **Erin**
- Host follow-ups route through **Kim**
- Batch host comms route through **Kim** directly from Ana

**Out of scope:** Technical implementation, system design, day-to-day host logistics.

---

### Garen — Technical Coordinator — Year 7

**Handles:**
- *Technical:* Systems, data, AV, IT. Film ingest, QC, print traffic. Host venue technical speccing, production planning and staffing.
- *Documentation:* Host guide, onboarding docs, technical specs. Process design, tools.
- *Advisement:* Language, cognitive load, accessibility, team empowerment through clear and useful systems.
- Routing — not sure where something goes? It starts here.

**Defines:** Technical approach, workflow design, inbox routing, onboarding materials. Autonomous unless it changes who does what.

**Greenlights:** Technical comms (film specs, filmmaker comms), data decisions, accessibility and technical requirements. Batch updates route through here.

**Routing:**
- Scope, budget, and people route through **Ana**
- Creative assets route through **Erin**

**Out of scope:** Fundraising, sponsor relationships, public-facing brand decisions, creative direction.

---

### Erin — Storyteller/Creative Assets — Year 9

**Handles:** Visual storytelling, creative assets, video production, trailer, screening slide decks, video documentation, filmmaker-in-residence, on-site documentation and technical backup during festival week.

**Defines:** Creative direction within specs, production choices, program design contour, how the story gets told.

**Greenlights:** Creative assets before they go out. Screening shape — look, feel, experiential flow.

**Routing:**
- Technical questions route through **Garen**
- Budget and scope route through **Ana**
- Coordinates with **Cesar** on look and feel
- Coordinates with **Garen** on communication standards

**Out of scope:** Systems infrastructure, host logistics, fundraising, technical specs.

---

### Kim — Host Comms Coordinator — Year 1

**Handles:** Host communications, documenting as she goes, identifying bottlenecks, translator between operations and strategy, identifies tasks that can route to interns or need documentation or automation.

**Defines:** Sends and follow-ups within the playbook. Growing toward operational architecture alongside Garen.

**Greenlights:** Routes through Garen or Ana.

**Routing:**
- Off-playbook questions route through **Garen**
- Budget and scope route through **Ana**
- Does not send comms outside the documented playbook

**Out of scope:** Creative direction, fundraising, technical infrastructure, scope decisions.

## Institutional memory

Ana (15 years), Erin (9), and Garen (7) hold the longest tenure. They keep the frame — not as managers or gatekeepers, but as people who remember why things work the way they do. When a choice affects how the whole team works, it runs through them before it reaches the wider group.

---

## Interns

Interns work within the area they're assigned to. Kim identifies and routes delegable tasks. Ana supervises.

---

## Open questions

- **Josh's greenlight boundary** — digital comms and day-of support is clear. What decisions can Josh make without checking with Ana?
- **Michael's event scope** — fundraising + Launch Party + Opening Night is clear. "Event planning" without specifying which events creates confusion.
- **Gap protocol** — when someone sees a gap, flag it. Right person picks it up, it goes out in the right voice.
