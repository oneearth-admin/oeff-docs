# Host Surveys — Stream 4

**Owner:** Kim (sends forms, monitors responses, flags issues)
**Backup:** Garen
**Tools:** Google Forms, Google Sheets, Mailmeteor, hosts@ Gmail, Claude (post-festival analysis)
**Audience:** Practitioner
**Reading context:** Desk-doing — activate each form at the right moment in the timeline

---

## What this is

Three Google Forms at different stages of the host journey. Each collects specific information needed for the next operational phase. Keep each form under 3 minutes to complete. Responses flow into Google Sheets for tracking and follow-up.

---

## The timeline

```
~3 weeks before screening (~Apr 1)    Form 1: Pre-Event Readiness
~2 days before screening (~Apr 20)    Form 2: Day-Of Confirmation
~2 days after festival (~Apr 29)      Form 3: Post-Screening Debrief
```

---

## Step by step

### 1. Build Form 1: Pre-Event Readiness — before first screening packet goes out

**What to do:**
1. Create a Google Form with these fields:
   - Confirm screening date and time
   - Venue contact name + phone for day-of emergencies
   - AV setup confirmation: "Have you tested the film file?" (Y / N / Not yet received)
   - T-shirt sizes (dropdown: S/M/L/XL, quantity)
   - Number of volunteers expected
   - Any special needs or questions (open text)
2. Set the form to collect email addresses (for matching responses to hosts)
3. Link the response sheet to V7 or create a new tab
4. Test the form yourself — fill it out, verify the response appears in the sheet

**Decision rights:** You own form design and deployment. If you want to add or remove fields, check with Garen — other processes may depend on specific fields (like t-shirt sizes feeding into an order).

---

### 2. Send Form 1 — ~3 weeks before screenings (~Apr 1)

**What to do:**
1. Draft a Mailmeteor email with the form link
2. Frame it as: "Help us make sure everything's ready for your screening"
3. Keep the email short — the form does the work
4. Send test, then send to warm host list
5. Set a reminder to check responses in 48 hours

**Decision rights:** You own the send.

---

### 3. Monitor Form 1 responses — ongoing after send

**What to do:**
1. Check the response sheet daily until all hosts have responded
2. Flag any "Not yet received" responses on the AV test question — these hosts may not have gotten their film file (route to Garen)
3. Flag any "Having issues" or concerning open-text responses to Garen
4. If a host hasn't responded within 5 days, send individual follow-up from hosts@
5. Compile t-shirt sizes and send to whoever handles ordering (route through Executive Director)

**Decision rights:** You own monitoring and follow-up. Route AV issues to Garen, budget items to Executive Director.

---

### 4. Build Form 2: Day-Of Confirmation — before Apr 20

**What to do:**
1. Create a Google Form — this one is extremely short:
   - Film plays correctly? (Y / N / Having issues)
   - Presenter/moderator confirmed? (Y / N / Need help)
   - Anything we should know? (open text)
2. Test the form
3. Verify: "Having issues" and "Need help" responses should trigger an immediate follow-up from you

**Decision rights:** You own form design. This form doesn't need approval — it's a safety net.

---

### 5. Send Form 2 — 2 days before each screening

**What to do:**
1. Draft Mailmeteor email — short, calm, reassuring: "Quick check before your screening"
2. Send to hosts whose screenings are in the next 3-4 days
3. If anyone selects "Having issues" or "Need help," respond within 2 hours during business hours

**Decision rights:** You own the send. If a host reports a tech issue, route to Garen immediately.

**If something goes wrong:** A host says their film won't play 2 days before their screening. This is urgent. Flag to Garen in Google Chat with "URGENT: [Venue Name] — film playback issue." He'll troubleshoot or arrange an alternative.

---

### 6. Build Form 3: Post-Screening Debrief — before Apr 29

**What to do:**
1. Create a Google Form:
   - Estimated attendance (number)
   - What went well? (open text)
   - What would you change? (open text)
   - Any technical issues? (Y / N + description)
   - Would you host again next year? (Yes / Maybe / No)
   - Photos to share? (file upload or link)
   - Anything else? (open text)
2. Gratitude framing at the top: "Thank you for hosting. Your feedback makes next year better."
3. Test the form

**Decision rights:** You own form design. Run the final version past Executive Director — debrief data shapes next year's decisions.

---

### 7. Send Form 3 + analyze results — ~Apr 29 and after

**What to do:**
1. Send via the thank-you + debrief email (Stream 3, Step 6)
2. Allow 2 weeks for responses
3. Follow up on non-respondents once (Stream 3, Step 7)
4. After collection closes, export responses to CSV
5. Feed the CSV to Claude and ask for:
   - Themes in "what went well" and "what would you change"
   - Technical issues grouped by venue
   - Flag any hosts who said "No" to hosting again — with their feedback
   - A draft "lessons learned" summary
6. Share the analysis with Garen and Executive Director

**Decision rights:** You own the analysis. The lessons-learned summary routes through Executive Director before it goes to the broader team.

## Escalation

- **Host reports film won't play (Form 2)** → Garen immediately. Tag "URGENT" in Google Chat.
- **Host says "Need help" with presenter (Form 2)** → Ask what they need. If it's a person, route to Executive Director.
- **Host says "No" to hosting again (Form 3)** → Don't follow up yourself. Flag to Executive Director with their feedback for a personal follow-up.
- **Low response rate on Form 3** → One follow-up is fine. After that, accept the data you have.

---

## Anchor questions

1. Were the forms the right length? Did any hosts comment on them being too long?
2. Did Form 2 catch any real issues? If not, was it still worth sending (peace of mind)?
3. What did the post-screening analysis reveal that surprised you? That's the most valuable signal.
