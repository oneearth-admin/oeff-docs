Decision-Rights Matrix

OEFF 2026 — Garen Hudson — Feb 16, 2026

Purpose: This matrix clarifies decision authority between the Executive Director and Technical Coordinator, reducing delays from ambiguous approval requirements. Each category defines who can act, what triggers escalation, and how decisions flow. The goal is fewer bottlenecks and faster execution during the pre-festival crunch.

How to Read This Matrix

Three authority levels:

  • Ana only — Requires Ana's explicit approval before action. Garen prepares options and recommendations.
  • Garen autonomous — Garen can decide and act, then inform Ana in the next weekly update or async check-in.
  • Shared — Either can initiate, but the other must confirm before execution. Quick text/email confirmation is sufficient.
Pilot year note: This is our first year operating in a distributed contractor model. These boundaries are proposed based on patterns observed during Nov 2025 – Feb 2026. Both parties should review and adjust after the festival retrospective.

Operating Principles

Default to action, not permission

For categories marked Garen autonomous, the expectation is: act first, inform after. Waiting for approval on routine execution creates the bottleneck this matrix is designed to prevent.

Escalation is not failure

Escalating a decision to Ana is a feature, not a bug. The threshold column defines when escalation is expected. Using it means the system is working.

Confirmation can be lightweight

For Shared decisions, confirmation does not require a meeting. A text reply of "yes" or "go ahead" is sufficient. The goal is alignment, not ceremony.

Weekly sync as safety net

The weekly 1:1 meeting catches anything that fell between categories. If Garen made an autonomous decision that Ana wants to revisit, the weekly meeting is the venue. No retroactive blame for good-faith autonomous action within these boundaries.
