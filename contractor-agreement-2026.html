This agreement formalizes the scope of work Garen Hudson has been performing for OEFF 2026 since November 2025, in ongoing coordination with the Executive Director. Both parties acknowledge that work began prior to formal contract execution and agree that pre-contract work is compensable under the same terms outlined here.

Scope of Work

The Technical Coordinator role spans two areas that have evolved together through OEFF's shift to a distributed contractor model. Both are part of this agreement.

Area 1: Technical Operations

  • Venue technical assessment and tier classification
  • AV planning and vendor coordination across venue tiers
  • Film file management, QC, and accessibility compliance
  • Day-of technical support coordination (remote)
  • Equipment and rental budgeting

Area 2: Systems & Coordination

  • Data architecture and spreadsheet infrastructure (V7 maintenance)
  • Communication platform migration (shared mailbox setup, adoption)
  • Team onboarding design (intake surveys, welcome materials, guides)
  • Process documentation and institutional knowledge capture
  • Host Guide and Docs Site development and maintenance
  • Contractor and volunteer recruitment, evaluation, and onboarding
  • Cross-team coordination and workflow design
Area 2 reflects work that emerged organically as OEFF transitioned from a single-person technical role to a distributed team. This scope was previously unformalized. Naming it here ensures visibility and prevents future scope ambiguity.

Scope Boundaries

Included

All work described above in Areas 1 and 2, including coordination with other OEFF contractors and volunteers in the technical domain.

Not included

  • On-site AV operation during festival week (covered by day-of team)
  • External marketing, social media, or public communications
  • Fundraising, grant writing, or donor management
  • Direct supervision of interns (coordination and onboarding support only)

Scope change process

If new work arises beyond this scope, Garen will flag it before beginning. If it can be absorbed within the budget cap, a brief acknowledgment from Ana via text, email, or verbal confirmation is sufficient. If it requires additional budget, both parties will discuss before work begins.

Collaboration Model

Availability

Garen holds full-time AV employment during weekday business hours. OEFF work happens on evenings (after 5pm) and weekends, with brief async check-ins possible during the workday. Garen will attend the Wednesday 11:45am squad meeting when schedule permits but cannot guarantee weekly attendance.

Communication

Primary channels: email and text for async coordination, with scheduled calls for decision-making. Live calls are preferred over document review for items requiring Ana's input or approval.

Delegation

Part of this role involves identifying work that can be delegated to other contractors or volunteers. When Garen recommends bringing in additional support, a brief conversation with Ana is the approval mechanism — not a formal proposal process. Subcontractor compensation is managed separately by OEFF from the collaborator pool and does not draw from this agreement's budget cap. Garen remains the primary point of coordination for any delegated work within the technical domain.

Compensation & Invoicing

Rate

$70 per hour, all work types. No distinction between Area 1 and Area 2 work.

Budget cap

$10,000 total for the contract term. When 75% of the cap ($7,500) has been invoiced, both parties will check in to review remaining scope and determine whether to adjust the cap, prioritize remaining work, or wind down the engagement.

Back-pay

Work performed from November 2025 through contract execution date is compensable at the agreed rate. Garen will submit an itemized hour log for this period within two weeks of signing.

Invoicing

Monthly invoices with brief descriptions of work completed, submitted by the 5th of the following month. Payment within 30 days of invoice receipt.

General Terms

Independent contractor status

Garen Hudson operates as an independent contractor, not an employee of One Earth Film Festival or One Earth Collective. Garen is responsible for their own taxes, insurance, and benefits. Nothing in this agreement creates an employer-employee relationship.

Work product

Deliverables created specifically for OEFF — including configured systems, organizational documentation, and data architecture containing OEFF data — are the property of One Earth Film Festival. Garen retains all rights to pre-existing tools, methodologies, frameworks, and generalized knowledge developed during the engagement. Garen may reference this work in professional portfolio materials, case studies, and future consulting engagements, provided that confidential organizational data (donor information, financial details, internal communications) is not disclosed. OEFF may be referenced by name as a client with prior approval of specific materials.

Termination

Either party may end this agreement with two weeks written notice. In the event of early termination, Garen will be compensated for all hours worked through the termination date, including any outstanding back-pay. Garen will provide reasonable transition support during the notice period.

Confidentiality

Garen will treat OEFF's internal data, donor information, and organizational communications as confidential. This does not restrict Garen from discussing general methodologies, approaches, or publicly available information about OEFF.

Transition

In the event this engagement concludes, Garen will ensure all systems and processes are documented sufficiently for another person to maintain them, and will transfer any credentials or access within 14 days. Reasonable transition support is included within the notice period.

Governing law

This agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Illinois. This engagement has been authorized within OEFF's board-approved budget for the 2026 festival season.

Pilot Year Acknowledgment

OEFF 2026 is operating under a new distributed contractor model. This is a pilot year. Both parties acknowledge that:

  • Roles and workflows are still being defined and may shift
  • This agreement reflects best current understanding of the work
  • Flexibility is expected and valued — but flexibility goes both directions
  • A post-festival retrospective will inform whether and how this role continues
