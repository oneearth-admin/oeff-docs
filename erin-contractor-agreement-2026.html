Coordination Model

Ana / Team Erin Garen | | | |------ meetings --------->| | | |--- notes/flags (async) --->| | | | | |<-- tasks to delegate ------| | | | | |--- contractor onboarding --| | | |

Erin handles

  • Real-time presence (meetings, same-day questions)
  • Contractor touchpoints (onboarding, check-ins)
  • Video production and editing decisions
  • Event capture logistics
  • Slide deck visual execution

Garen handles

  • Process design, documentation, specs
  • Complex technical decisions
  • Contractor scoping and SOW creation
  • Escalation endpoint
The handoff: Garen creates the systems (templates, onboarding docs, escalation triggers). Erin operates within them (uses the templates, runs the onboarding, follows the triggers). Both update the shared dashboard so neither is chasing the other for status.

Scope Boundaries

Included

All work described in Areas 1 and 2, including coordination with other OEFF contractors and the Technical Coordinator.

Not included

  • Technical specs (file formats, QC standards, equipment requirements)
  • Process design or workflow architecture
  • Contractor scoping or SOW creation
  • Escalation decisions beyond documented triggers
  • Equipment budgeting or vendor selection

Scope change process

If new work arises beyond this scope, Erin will flag it before beginning. If it can be absorbed within the budget cap, a brief acknowledgment from Ana via text, email, or verbal confirmation is sufficient. If it requires additional budget, both parties will discuss before work begins.

Compensation & Invoicing

Rate

$40 per hour, all work types. No distinction between creative and operations work.

Budget cap

$4,600 total for the contract term. When 75% of the cap (~$3,450) has been invoiced, both parties will check in to review remaining scope and priorities.

Invoicing

Monthly invoices with brief descriptions of work completed, submitted by the 5th of the following month. Payment within 30 days of invoice receipt.

General Terms

Independent contractor status

Erin Turney & Co operates as an independent contractor, not an employee of One Earth Film Festival or One Earth Collective. Erin is responsible for her own taxes, insurance, and benefits. Nothing in this agreement creates an employer-employee relationship.

Work product

Deliverables created specifically for OEFF — including video content, slide decks, branded materials, and event documentation — are the property of One Earth Film Festival. Erin retains all rights to pre-existing tools, techniques, and generalized knowledge. Erin may reference this work in professional portfolio materials and future engagements, provided that confidential organizational data is not disclosed. OEFF may be referenced by name as a client with prior approval of specific materials.

Termination

Either party may end this agreement with two weeks written notice. In the event of early termination, Erin will be compensated for all hours worked through the termination date. Erin will provide reasonable transition support during the notice period, including handoff of any in-progress deliverables.

Confidentiality

Erin will treat OEFF's internal data, donor information, and organizational communications as confidential. This does not restrict Erin from discussing general methodologies, approaches, or publicly available information about OEFF.

Governing law

This agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Illinois. This engagement has been authorized within OEFF's board-approved budget for the 2026 festival season.

Pilot Year Acknowledgment

OEFF 2026 is operating under a new distributed contractor model. This is a pilot year. Both parties acknowledge that:

  • Roles and workflows are still being defined and may shift
  • This agreement reflects best current understanding of the work
  • Flexibility is expected and valued — but flexibility goes both directions
  • A post-festival retrospective will inform whether and how this role continues
