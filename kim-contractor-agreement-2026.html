Festival Week On-Site Support (April 22-27) — Proposed

Role

Kim serves as on-site Stage / Production Manager for host venue screenings during festival week. She is the primary point of contact for hosts on the ground during screenings.

Responsibilities

  • Run of show coordination. Communicate final ROS to hosts in advance. Walk hosts through the flow on arrival. Ensure each screening starts and ends as planned.
  • Host check-ins. Afternoon arrival at assigned venue(s). Confirm host readiness, resolve last-minute questions, and ensure hosts have what they need before doors open.
  • Program team relay. Pick up and relay program team decisions to hosts in real time — schedule changes, speaker updates, audience flow adjustments.
  • Intern coordination. Serve as first line of contact for interns assigned to screenings. Delegate tasks, answer questions, route issues to the appropriate team lead.
  • People wrangling. Interface with venue tech teams, manage audience flow at the door, and handle the human logistics that keep a screening running smoothly.
  • Escalation. Route technical issues to Garen (remote) or the on-site AV team. Route programming issues to Ana or Josh.

Pre-fest-week prep (included)

  • Attend Monday host meetings in the weeks leading up to the festival
  • Review final ROS documents for assigned venues
  • Coordinate with Garen on venue-specific technical requirements
  • Confirm intern assignments and brief interns on their roles

Schedule

All 6 festival days: April 22 (Opening Night) through April 27.

Daily window: afternoon through beginning of screening (~4-6 hours/day depending on venue and event complexity). Heavier days are Thu-Sat when multiple host sites are active simultaneously.

Coordination Model

Kim handles:

  • Day-to-day host communications execution (mail merges, reminders, follow-ups)
  • Outreach to gather needed info and host deliverables at various points in time
  • Host inbox triage and response
  • Process documentation from the operator's perspective
  • Filmmaker communications (stretch goal)

Garen handles:

  • Communication plan design, templates, and automation specs
  • Escalation endpoint for complex host questions
  • QC on outgoing communications
  • Scoping and prioritization of Kim's work

The handoff:

  • Garen creates the systems (templates, dependency maps, mail merge infrastructure)
  • Kim operates within them (runs the merges, sends the emails, monitors responses)
  • Kim documents what's missing or broken so the systems improve over time

As Kim builds context, the boundary between "operates" and "shapes" naturally shifts. The scope of what she owns is expected to widen over the engagement — starting with execution, growing into identifying delegable tasks, proposing workflow improvements, and coordinating with interns as capacity allows.
