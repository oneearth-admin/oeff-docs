Two separate websites, one backend. Here's who sees what:

Two sites, two audiences

Host Guide hosts.oneearthfilmfest.org. For hosts — webinars, action cards, general resources. Same for everyone.

What we call things: When talking to hosts, say "Your Screening Page." Internally, we say "venue page" or just reference the venue name. The old "Host Helpers" term is retired. And "Your Screening Page" always means the host-side page — never the public event listing on Squarespace.

5. Phasing

We don't need 100% data to start. The principle: breadcrumb, don't gate. Give hosts something useful now. Add detail as it arrives.

Phase 1 — Now (this week)
Team sign-off on page format. Generate screening pages for all 22 venues. Share a few with Ana/Josh for review before rolling out URLs to hosts.
Phase 2 — Mid-March
Send intake form to all 26 hosts (Kim). Responses flow into Airtable. Pages start filling in with host-provided data.
Phase 3 — Late March
Share screening page URLs with hosts. Each host bookmarks their page. Follow up on non-responders.
Phase 4 — April (pre-festival)
QA pass — all pages have film, time, AV, contacts. Day-of sections go live. Screening packets added after film delivery.

7. Kim's Trackers

Kim is building two tracking tools — a Communications Plan (what gets sent, when, to whom) and an Engagement Tracker (who responded, who needs follow-up). Here's how they connect:

Tool What It Tracks Owner
Kim's Comms Plan What was sent, when, which campaign, which template Kim
Kim's Engagement Tracker Who opened, who replied, who needs a nudge Kim
Airtable The facts — film, date, venue details, intake responses Team
Screening Pages The output — what hosts actually see and use Garen

These aren't competing systems — they're complementary. Kim's trackers answer "did we reach them?" Airtable answers "what do we know?" Screening pages answer "what does the host need to do?"
