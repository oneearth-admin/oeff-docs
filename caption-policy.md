# OEFF 2026 Caption Policy

**For:** Filmmakers submitting to One Earth Film Festival 2026
**From:** OEFF Technical Team
**Last updated:** 2026-03-12

---

Every OEFF screening is captioned. This is a core accessibility commitment — no exceptions, no opt-outs.

We want to make this as easy as possible for you. Here's what we need and how we'll help.

## What to Send Us

**Option A: You have an SRT file**
Send us the `.srt` file alongside your final video. We'll run quality checks, and if anything needs fixing (timing drift, formatting), we'll handle it. Your film, your captions — we just make sure they're ready for the screen.

- **Format:** SRT (SubRip) is preferred. WebVTT is also accepted — we'll convert it.
- **Encoding:** UTF-8. If your file has accented characters or non-Latin text, UTF-8 ensures nothing breaks.
- **Timing:** Captions must be synced to the exact video file you deliver. If you send us a re-edited version later, the captions will need to be re-synced. (This is the single biggest source of problems — see "The Caption Lock" below.)

**Option B: Your captions are already burned into the video**
Some films arrive with open captions baked into the image. That works. We'll spot-check readability and timing, but there's nothing else you need to send.

**Option C: You don't have captions**
We'll get them made. Here's how cost works:

- **Paid screening (licensing fee):** OEFF commissions captions through Rev. The cost is deducted from your licensing fee. You keep the SRT file — it's yours to use however you want after the festival.
- **Free screening (fee waived):** OEFF covers the caption cost. You still keep the SRT file.

Either way, you walk away with a captioned version of your film. That's the deal.

## The Caption Lock

Captions must match the final cut of your film. If you're still editing, wait to caption. If you send us a final cut and then make changes, the captions will drift — words will appear too early or too late, and it gets worse as the film goes on.

**The rule:** Declare your video final before captioning begins. If you need to make changes after that, let us know — we'll re-sync or re-commission the captions. Don't assume old captions fit a new edit.

## Caption Specs (for those who want the details)

| Spec | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Format | SRT (primary), WebVTT (accepted) |
| Encoding | UTF-8 (with or without BOM) |
| Max lines per block | 2 |
| Max characters per line | ~32 |
| Minimum display time | 1 second |
| Maximum display time | ~6 seconds |
| Speaker identification | Required when multiple speakers are on screen |
| Sound descriptions | Required for significant non-speech audio (e.g., `[applause]`, `[music]`) |

You don't need to memorize these. If you send us an SRT file, we'll check it against these specs and fix anything that's off.

## Timeline

Send your caption file (or let us know you need help) when you deliver your final video. The earlier, the better — captioning through Rev takes 1-3 business days, and we're working across 22 venues.

Reach out to [OEFF technical contact]. We'd rather answer a question now than troubleshoot a sync issue at the venue.
