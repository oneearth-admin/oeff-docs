Host Communications Plan

OEFF 2026 — Garen Hudson — Feb 12, 2026

Guiding Principles

Research on nonprofit event communication cadence consistently finds three things:

  1. Anticipate needs, don't react to them. Hosts should receive information before they realize they need it. Reminders 2 days before a webinar, not the morning of.
  2. Ramp frequency as the event approaches. Monthly in planning phase, weekly in the month before, daily in festival week. (SchoolAuction research)
  3. Separate channels by purpose. Email for scheduled communications. Host guide for reference. Google Form for data collection. Don't make hosts hunt. (Cloud Nine research)
The rule: Every email should either (a) tell hosts to do something specific, or (b) give them something they'll need soon. No emails that are just "updates." If there's nothing actionable, don't send.

Communication Streams

There are five distinct streams of host communication, each with its own rhythm and tooling.

Stream 1: Webinar Reminders + Recording Follow-ups

Cadence

For each of the 5 webinars + 2 optional office hours:

  • 7 days before: Preview email via Mailmeteor (template 03). Topic preview, agenda, prep notes.
  • 1 day before: Reminder email via Mailmeteor (template 04). Short nudge + Zoom link.
  • 1 day after: Recap + recording email via Mailmeteor (template 05). Recording link, key takeaways, next session preview.
  • Same day as follow-up: Update host guide with recording link (add to the "Past recordings" section)

Implementation

  1. Templates live in email-drafts/ — 03 (preview), 04 (reminder), 05 (recap). Each includes per-webinar content blocks.
  2. Batch rhythm: Sunday/Monday: send preview (7 days out) + reminder (1 day out). Tuesday: send recap with recording link.
  3. Host guide update: After each webinar, add the recording link to the recordings section in index.html. Git push auto-deploys.
  4. Process doc: See kim-webinar-cycle-process.md for the full step-by-step cycle Kim follows.

AI support

Use Claude to draft a 2-3 sentence recap of each webinar's key points for the follow-up email. Feed it the recording or your notes; it generates the summary. This saves 15-20 min per follow-up.

Implementation

  1. Add columns to the warm host sheet: Film Delivered (Y/N), Promo Sent (Y/N), Slides Sent (Y/N), Presenter Guide Sent (Y/N), T-shirt Size, T-shirt Shipped (Y/N)
  2. Draft 3 Mailmeteor templates:
    • "Your screening packet is ready" — film file + promo materials (3 weeks out)
    • "Slides and trailers for your screening" — Google Slides deck (1 week out)
    • "Final check-in before your screening" — day-of logistics confirmation (day before)
  3. Personalize with merge fields: {{Film Title}}, {{Screening Date}}, {{Download Link}}, {{Venue Name}}
  4. Use Mailmeteor's tracking to confirm opens. If a host doesn't open the screening packet email within 48 hours, follow up individually.

AI support

Use Claude to generate per-host download instructions based on their venue tier. Tier 1 (full-service) gets different instructions than Tier 3 (self-sufficient). Feed Claude the tier and venue details; it generates the right version.

Stream 4: Collecting From Hosts (Surveys)

Three Google Forms at different stages. Keep each under 3 minutes. Responses flow into Google Sheets for tracking.

Form 1: Pre-Event Readiness (send ~3 weeks before)

Purpose: Confirm logistics and collect what you need for deliverables.

  • Confirm screening date and time
  • Venue contact name + phone for day-of
  • AV setup confirmation: "Have you tested the film file?" (Y/N/Not yet received)
  • T-shirt sizes (dropdown: S/M/L/XL, quantity)
  • Number of volunteers expected
  • Any special needs or questions (open text)
Google Form Sent via Mailmeteor

Form 2: Day-Of Confirmation (send 2 days before)

Purpose: Final gut-check. Extremely short.

  • Film plays correctly? (Y/N/Having issues)
  • Presenter/moderator confirmed? (Y/N/Need help)
  • Anything we should know? (open text)
Google Form Sent via Mailmeteor

Form 3: Post-Screening Debrief (send 2 days after)

Purpose: Capture what happened for next year. Gratitude first, data second.

  • Estimated attendance
  • What went well? (open text)
  • What would you change? (open text)
  • Any technical issues? (Y/N + description)
  • Would you host again next year? (Yes / Maybe / No)
  • Photos to share? (file upload or link)
  • Anything else? (open text)
Google Form Sent via Mailmeteor Claude analysis

AI support for surveys

After the festival, export all Form 3 responses to a CSV. Feed it to Claude and ask for:

  • Themes in "what went well" and "what would you change" (cluster analysis)
  • Technical issues by venue tier (are Tier 1 venues having different problems than Tier 3?)
  • A draft "lessons learned" summary for the team
  • Flag any hosts who said "No" to hosting again — with their feedback, for Ana to follow up personally

Stream 5: Cool Host Conversion

Separate from the warm host streams. These are venues that expressed interest but haven't committed or selected a film.

The conversion email (next week)

Short, clear, one ask: fill out this form.

  • Acknowledge the gap since last contact ("We know it's been a few weeks")
  • Link to a film selection form (Google Form with embedded film list PDF link)
  • Offer a quick call if they want help choosing
  • Link to host guide for reference

Film selection form

  • Your name + venue name
  • Link to film list PDF (view trailers, read synopses)
  • Top 1-2 film choices (text field — they type the title)
  • "Do you need to see the full film before deciding?" (Y/N)
  • Preferred screening date (date picker or text)
  • Any questions? (open text)
Google Form Sent via Mailmeteor

When a cool host fills out this form and selects a film, move them to the warm host sheet. They now enter the regular communication streams.

Implementation Plan

Phase 1: Infrastructure (this week + next)

  1. Warm host sheet — add columns: Film Title, Screening Date, Venue Tier, Film Delivered, Promo Sent, Slides Sent, T-shirt Size, T-shirt Shipped
  2. Cool host sheet — create separate sheet. Columns: Name, Email, Venue, Status (Contacted / Form Sent / Responded / Converted)
  3. Draft all email templates — use Claude to generate drafts, then refine for tone. Templates needed:
    • Webinar reminder (reusable, swap {{Topic}} and {{Date}})
    • Webinar recording follow-up (reusable, swap recording link + summary)
    • Cool host conversion email
    • Film confirmation (per-host)
    • Marketing kit ready
    • Screening packet delivery
    • Slides/trailers delivery
    • One week out
    • Eve of festival
    • Thank you + debrief
  4. Build 3 Google Forms — pre-event readiness, day-of confirmation, post-screening debrief
  5. Build film selection form for cool hosts

Phase 2: Weekly rhythm (ongoing Feb-Apr)

  1. Monday AM: Check the calendar. What goes out this week?
  2. Monday AM: Schedule that week's Mailmeteor sends (reminders, follow-ups)
  3. Tuesday after webinar: Record, upload, send recap, update host guide, git push to deploy
  4. Sunday/Monday: Send preview + reminder for next webinar
  5. Weekly: Check warm host sheet for any missing confirmations or deliverables. Follow up individually.

Phase 3: Post-festival (late Apr - May)

  1. Send debrief survey (Form 3)
  2. Export responses, feed to Claude for theme analysis
  3. Draft lessons-learned doc for the team
  4. Update the runbook with anything new
  5. Archive the 2026 comms calendar as a template for 2027
Where AI fits in this plan: Claude drafts every email template (saves ~30 min each, 10 templates = 5 hours saved). Claude summarizes webinar recordings for follow-ups. Claude analyzes post-screening survey data. Claude generates per-host personalized instructions based on venue tier. Total AI-assisted time savings: ~10-15 hours across the festival cycle.
Human-in-the-loop by design: Mailmeteor doesn't do true automation (no drip sequences, no conditional "if not opened, re-send"). Each send is manual or scheduled. The weekly rhythm in Phase 2 compensates — Kim follows the calendar, preps and sends. This is intentional: host comms benefit from human judgment on timing and tone.
