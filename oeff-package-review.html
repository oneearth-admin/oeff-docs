Fresh-Eyes Audit: Host Communications & Process Documentation

OEFF 2026 — Starter project for Kim

Why this project

OEFF has been building documentation, communication templates, and host-facing resources at speed — but almost entirely from the inside. Everything was written by people who already know the org. We've never had someone with fresh eyes and a process background look at what we've built and tell us what's clear, what's confusing, and what's missing.

This project gives Kim a defined first contribution with a real deliverable, while also getting her oriented to OEFF's model, team, and workflows. She learns the org by reading the org.

What you'll review

Primary materials

  • Host Guide (hosts.oneearthfilmfest.org) — the main resource hosts receive for screening logistics, setup, and support
  • Team Documentation site (docs.oneearthfilmfest.org) — internal team reference, processes, and onboarding info
  • Host communication templates — email scripts and outreach language currently in use or in draft

Secondary context (provided by Garen)

  • Venue tier system overview — how OEFF classifies and supports different venue types
  • Brief team roster — who does what, in broad strokes
  • The shared mailbox migration plan — where email communication is heading

The ask

Review each resource as if you're encountering OEFF for the first time. Write up your observations in whatever format feels natural. The goal is honest signal, not a polished report.

Three questions to anchor the audit

1. What's unclear? Where would a new host, volunteer, or team member get stuck?

2. What's missing? Are there obvious gaps — steps that aren't documented, decisions that aren't explained?

3. What could be systematized? Where do you see repeated patterns that could become templates, checklists, or standard sequences?

Live track: Host comms observation

In addition to the document review, we'll loop you into host communications as they happen during your audit window. No action required from you — just observe and note what you see.

What you'll be watching

OEFF communicates with hosts through three streams, all of which will be active during your review:

  • Webinar reminders & follow-ups — 5 Monday noon sessions (started Feb 23) plus 2 optional office hours. Each webinar gets a Mailmeteor reminder 2 days before and a recording follow-up the day after. You'll see both the sends and any replies that come back.
  • Deliverables shipping — promotional materials, film files, and screening packets go out to hosts on a countdown schedule (3 weeks, 2 weeks, 1 week before screening). You'll see the coordination emails and any host questions.
  • Inbound host questions — hosts email hosts@oneearthfilmfest.org with setup questions, scheduling issues, tech concerns. You'll see the incoming traffic and how it gets routed or answered.

How it works technically

  • We'll add you as a reader on the shared inboxes (hosts@, tech@) so you see traffic without being in the reply chain
  • Mailmeteor bulk sends go from hosts@ — you'll see both outbound sends and individual replies
  • Garen will flag anything notable in a quick async message ("watch this thread — good example of escalation")

What to watch for

  • Where do hosts get confused? What questions come up repeatedly?
  • Does the tone and language match what's in the docs and templates?
  • How long does it take for questions to get answered? Who answers them?
  • Are there gaps between what the host guide says and what actually happens in email?

This serves two purposes: it gives you live context for your audit (you're not just reading static docs — you're seeing the system in motion), and it gives you a natural entry point into host communication work if we continue past Phase 1.
