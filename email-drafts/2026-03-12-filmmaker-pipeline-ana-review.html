▸Screener vs. exhibition file — the rule: Screeners are preview copies for programming committees. They often have watermarks, lower resolution (720p), or timecode overlays. Never use a screener as the actual screening file. It signals to filmmakers that we're not treating their work professionally, and watermarks are visible to the audience. Always request the exhibition-quality file (ProRes or H.264, 1080p+, no watermarks) via Dropbox upload.

What screeners are good for right now: Checking caption fidelity. We can watch the Vimeo screeners for Beyond Zero and In Our Nature to assess whether the dialogue is cleanly captionable (accents, overlapping speech, music-heavy sections). This informs our caption planning before files arrive.
