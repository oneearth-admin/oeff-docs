Thanks for flagging, Michael. Here's what happened, what's fixed, and how to handle any supporter messages.

What happened

The early access email included a link to /films-by-date, which was still pointing to the 2024 page. A few supporters landed on old content. This has been fixed — I set up a redirect so that link now lands on the correct 2026 page. No resend needed. The links in everyone's inbox work correctly now.

I also removed the per-film Squarespace passwords and the Eventbrite event passwords. The /films-by-date listing page still has its password (OEFF2026) as the single gate — once supporters enter it, they can browse film pages and grab tickets without entering another code.

If a supporter writes in

  • Keep it simple: "Thanks for letting us know — we had a brief technical issue that's been resolved. Please try the link again and you should land on the right page."
  • Don't over-explain. No need to walk them through what went wrong on the backend. Most people just want to know it works now.
  • Don't overapologize. A quick "thanks for flagging" is enough. This was a minor glitch, not a crisis — treating it like one signals more severity than it warrants.
  • Most supporters won't have noticed. Out of 16k+ recipients, only a small fraction will have clicked through during the window when the link was wrong. Respond to anyone who reaches out, but don't send a correction blast.

Preventing this next time

For any future email that goes to our full list, I'd like us to add a validator step before hitting send: one or two people click every link, complete the full flow (email → page → ticket), and confirm it works. This takes 10 minutes and catches exactly the kind of thing that happened here.

We've already started doing this with host comms after a couple of earlier hiccups, and it's been working well. Same principle — when the audience is this large, a quick sanity check from a second pair of eyes is worth it.

Happy to set up a simple checklist for this if that's helpful.
