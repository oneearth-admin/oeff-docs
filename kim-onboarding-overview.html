AI Onboarding Tool Options

Kim needs a way to ask questions about OEFF processes, team structure, and history. Here are four approaches, from most capable to simplest.

Google NotebookLM

Recommended

Free

Upload the 12 onboarding docs. Kim gets a conversational AI that answers questions grounded in your actual documentation. She can ask "how does host onboarding work?" and get answers with citations back to the source docs.

Works well for
  • Free, no subscriptions
  • Kim only needs a Google account
  • Grounded in your docs (less hallucination)
  • Shareable — you and Kim both access it
  • No account sharing or ToS issues
Limitations
  • Not Claude (Gemini-based)
  • Less nuanced reasoning
  • 50-source limit per notebook
  • Can't set custom system instructions

Claude Pro + Shared Project

$20/mo

$20/mo for your account (you may already have this)

Create a Project on your Claude Pro account. Upload docs, paste the custom instructions we drafted. Share the project link with Kim — she'd need her own free or Pro Claude account to use it.

Works well for
  • Best reasoning quality (it's Claude)
  • Custom system instructions
  • Project knowledge persists across chats
  • If you already have Pro, no added cost for you
Limitations
  • Kim needs her own Claude account
  • Free tier has limited daily usage
  • Project sharing is limited — she may not be able to access your project directly without her own Pro sub

Claude Team Plan

$125+/mo

$25/user/mo (annual) × 5 seat minimum = $125/mo

Full shared workspace with collaborative Projects, admin console, and higher usage limits. Everyone on the team gets access to the same knowledge base.

Works well for
  • True collaboration — shared Projects, shared chats
  • Best AI quality + highest usage limits
  • Admin controls
  • Could serve the whole OEFF team long-term
Limitations
  • 5-seat minimum is overkill for onboarding one person
  • $1,500/year commitment
  • Hard to justify in current budget

Google Drive Folder + Reading Guide

Free

Free

A shared Google Drive folder with the 12 docs organized by reading order, plus a one-page "start here" guide that explains what each file is, why it matters, and what to look for. No AI, just good information architecture.

Works well for
  • Zero cost, zero friction
  • Kim already uses Google Drive
  • You control exactly what she sees
  • No tool onboarding needed
Limitations
  • Not interactive — she reads, not asks
  • Harder to find specific answers in 12 docs
  • No conversational exploration
Recommendation: Start with NotebookLM + Google Drive folder. Free, fast to set up, and Kim gets both the interactive layer (ask questions) and the reading layer (structured path). If she needs deeper Claude access later, you can add a Pro account then.

Kim's Onboarding Path

Before the call

Set up the infrastructure so Kim has everything on day one.

  1. Create a Google Drive folder called "OEFF — Kim Onboarding" with the 12 docs below
  2. Create a NotebookLM notebook and upload the same docs
  3. Send the proposal .docx (kim-onboarding-proposal.docx) with a short personal note
  4. Schedule the kickoff call — you, Ana, Kim, 30 min, week of Feb 17

The kickoff call (30 min)

Don't walk through every doc. Instead:

  1. 5 min — Intros, how Kim got here, what excites her
  2. 10 min — The org in plain language: who does what, how we communicate, where things break
  3. 10 min — Walk through the Drive folder and NotebookLM: "Here's everything. Start with these 3 docs. The AI tool can answer questions as you go."
  4. 5 min — Align on Phase 1 deliverables and first check-in (1 week out)

Week 1: Kim reads, asks, audits

She works through the reading list at her own pace (~7 hours). Uses NotebookLM to clarify things. Starts forming her audit observations. You're available async for questions.

Week 2: Kim delivers the audit + onboarding packet draft

Knowledge base audit: what exists, what's missing, what's inaccessible. Draft onboarding packet and delegation playbook. You and Ana review.

Week 3: Training + transition to Phase 2

Kim facilitates 1–2 training sessions with the team. Then transitions into host support and comms routing work as the festival ramps up.

Operational Takeaways from Ana Call

Key items from the Feb 12 conversation that aren't captured elsewhere.

Communication flow

  • Caesar doesn't check Spaces. Route technical/marketing asks through Garen, who can bullet out specifics and hand off directly.
  • Not everyone needs to see everything. Caesar doesn't need AV details for Field Museum. Hosts don't need internal catering threads. Information should be directed.
  • Contractor communication expectations need to be sensitive to scope — ad hoc systems can't be imposed on folks coming in for specific deliverables.

Templates needed

  • Trailer reel template: Typeface, logo placement, overlay position, opacity. "Use exactly what we did last year" — needs to be documented so it's not re-decided every cycle.
  • Marketing asset request format: The preview party back-and-forth was clunky. Needs clear specs, deadlines, and approval flow.
  • Captioning specs: Note in the request when something needs captions, and which version to caption (final, not draft).

Host pipeline

  • Warm hosts — confirmed, have selected films, get the host guide + webinar registration today.
  • Cool hosts — expressed interest but haven't committed or selected a film. Need a separate, shorter email with a film selection form. Going out early next week.
  • Film selection form idea: Integrate the film list into a Google Form so hosts can browse and select in one place. Link to a PDF of the film list (not the live internal sheet).

Staffing

  • Interns are all paid — UIC (Sustainability Institute), Dominican University (career placement). Never unpaid.
  • On-site gap: Garen and Erin won't be on-site much during the fest. Need bodies for flagship + support. Kim might fill part of this.
  • Lead time: An extra week on deliverables would have prevented most of the preview party friction. Build buffer into future timelines.
