## Scope of Work

The Technical Coordinator role spans two areas that have evolved together through OEFF's shift to a distributed contractor model. Both are part of this agreement.

### Area 1: Technical Operations

- Venue technical assessment and scope analysis
- AV planning and vendor coordination across venue tiers
- Film file management, QC, and accessibility compliance
- Print traffic planning, technical accessibility coordination — open captioned files, captioning of trailer reel
- Equipment and rental budgeting

### Area 2: Systems & Coordination

- Data architecture and infrastructure
- Communication platform migration (shared mailbox setup, adoption)
- Team onboarding design (intake surveys, welcome materials, guides)
- Process documentation and institutional knowledge capture
- Host Guide and Docs Site development and maintenance
- Contractor recruitment, evaluation, and onboarding
- Cross-team coordination and workflow design

## Scope Boundaries

### Included

All work described above in Areas 1 and 2, including coordination with other OEFF contractors and volunteers in the technical domain.

### Not included

- On-site AV operation during festival week (covered by day-of team)
- External marketing, social media, or public communications
- Fundraising, grant writing, or donor management
- Direct supervision of interns (coordination and onboarding support only)

### Scope change process

If new work arises beyond this scope, the Contractor will flag it before beginning. If it can be absorbed within the budget cap, a brief acknowledgment from the Executive Director via text, email, or verbal confirmation is sufficient. If it requires additional budget, both parties will discuss before work begins.

## Collaboration Model

### Availability

The Contractor is available evenings (after 5pm) and weekends, with brief async check-ins possible during the workday. The Contractor will attend the Wednesday 11:45am squad meeting when schedule permits but cannot guarantee weekly attendance. The Contractor and the Executive Director hold a weekly Tuesday 1:1 for decision-making and prioritization.

### Delegation

Part of this role involves identifying work that can be delegated to other contractors or volunteers. When the Contractor recommends bringing in additional support, a brief conversation with the Executive Director is the approval mechanism — not a formal proposal process. Subcontractor compensation is managed separately by OEFF from the collaborator pool and does not draw from this agreement's budget cap.

It is understood that all technical operations budget is capped at $20,000. Current allocations within that cap: the Creative Assets Lead (Erin Turney Co) at ~$5,490, and Host Communications Coordinator (Kim Pham) at ~$1,920 pre-festival (festival week on-site support proposed but scoped separately). The remaining ~$2,590 is a flexible pool for day-of staffing, on-site planning support, and equipment or tools as needed. Equipment overhead is minimal this year given OEFF's partnership-based approach and reduced production services scope.

The Contractor remains the primary point of coordination for any delegated work within the technical domain.

## Compensation & Invoicing

### Rate

$70 per hour, all work types. No distinction between Area 1 and Area 2 work.

### Budget cap

$10,000 total for the contract term. When 75% of the cap ($7,500) has been invoiced, both parties will check in to review remaining scope and determine whether to adjust the cap, prioritize remaining work, or wind down the engagement.

### Back-pay

Work performed from November 2025 through contract execution date is compensable at the agreed rate. These hours are included in the estimated total above. The Contractor will submit an itemized hour log for this period within two weeks of signing.

### Invoicing

Monthly invoices submitted to finance@oneearthcollective.org with brief descriptions of work completed, submitted by the 5th of the following month. Payment within 30 days of invoice receipt.
