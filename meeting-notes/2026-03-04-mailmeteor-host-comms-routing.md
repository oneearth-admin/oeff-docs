## What Surfaced in the Meeting

### Tool overlap: Mailmeteor vs Mailchimp

Josh is already using Mailchimp for some sends. Kim is set up on Mailmeteor. Two people sending from overlapping lists with different tools is a recipe for duplicate emails and confused tracking.

**Question for Ana:** Are we over-engineering this? Do we consolidate on one tool, or are these genuinely different channels that benefit from different tools? If so, which sends go through which tool — and who owns each?

### Reply routing: who responds to what?

Josh is still fielding host replies that may now be Kim's domain. No one has a clear picture of:

- Who responds to host questions?
- Who gets CC'd on replies?
- What happens when a reply comes back to the group alias — who picks it up?

The "reply-from" address is also not visible to people who are members of the group, which adds confusion.

**This needs a simple routing table** — not a policy doc, just a shared understanding of "if a host asks about X, Y person responds."

### Approval authority: what needs Ana sign-off?

We need clarity on the line between:

- **Sends Kim/Josh can do autonomously** (routine updates, reminders, logistical info)
- **Sends that need Ana's eyes first** (anything new, anything that sets expectations, anything that touches money or dates)

Without this, either everything stalls waiting for approval, or things go out that shouldn't have.

**Group alias (hosts@) replies:** Kim triages. If it's clearly in someone else's lane, she forwards with a heads-up — doesn't need to respond first.

**CC principle:** CC the person who needs awareness, not the person who needs to act. If someone needs to act, they're the responder, not the CC.

## Proposed: Approval Tiers

What can Kim and Josh send without Ana's sign-off?

### Tier 1 — Send autonomously (no approval needed)

- Webinar recap emails (like the one Kim just sent)
- Reminder emails for things already announced (intake form follow-ups, registration reminders)
- Logistical updates with no new information (Zoom link reminders, calendar updates)
- Thank-you and acknowledgment replies to individual hosts

### Tier 2 — Needs Ana's eyes before send

- Any email that announces something for the first time (new dates, new expectations, schedule changes)
- Emails that set or change deadlines
- Anything that touches money, contracts, or commitments
- Emails to the full host list that aren't routine updates
- Anything where you're unsure — when in doubt, Tier 2

### Tier 3 — Ana sends directly

- Per-film breakout call outreach
- Relationship-sensitive conversations (hosts who are unhappy, confused, or at risk of dropping)
- Partnership-level communications (sponsors, institutional partners)

**The test:** "If a host replied with a question I can't answer, would I forward it to Ana anyway?" If yes, it's probably Tier 2 or 3.
